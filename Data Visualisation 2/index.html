Melbourne property prices by time of sale

Property prices are typically heavily dependent on their proximity to the city and other places, such as beaches, schools, parks, hospitals and other attractions. The visualisation suggests properties close to the ocean are considerably more valuable.

While Melbourne is the largest city in the area, it could be possible for prices surrounding some of the smaller cities, such as Cranbourne (population 500k) to have a similar proximity effect on prices. Clearly, this is not the case, proximity to local cities is not a contributing factor to price.

It is evident that the time of sale can greatly affect the sale price of a property. Over the period 2016-2018, July was a period of poor real estate performance each year, and performance increasing quickly after. The season can greatly affect a buyer's decision making, hence, July, in the middle of winter with shorter days and colder weather proves a poor time to sell property. 

It is also evident that a strong market can last long periods of time and that there is considerable correlation between the number of houses sold and average house prices.

Real estate agents

The effect of land size and rooms on sale price
How influential are seller's agents?

There is a strong relationship between distance to CBD, number of rooms, land size and price. 

Properties close to the city are likely to be smaller in terms of their number of rooms and land size; but fetch the same or higher prices than properties farther from the city.

There is significant variation in performance of different real estate agents. 

The strongest agency (Hodges) averages a sale price of over 200% of Professionals, the worst performer. Consequently, the selling agent of a property is influential on the sale price. 

Distance to Melbourne's CBD

Property Price against distance to Melbourne CBD

The expected trend of property prices increasing as they are closer to the CBD is present, however, there are some prevalent outliers. 

In particular, at 15.2km from the city, prices spike. This is the beachside suburb of Sandringham . It's high prices are driven by its vicinity to Mentone Grammar (prestigious private school) and ocean views. 

The spike at 7.7km represents Camberwell, another suburb with a prominent private school (Camberwell Grammar school) and excellent access to public transport and the CBD. 

Do price fluctuations affect some places more than others?

Average prices by capital cities and non-capital city areas

Real estate experts suggest that more expensive properties are most heavily affected by changes in the market. 

In 2008, as a result of the Global Financial Crisis, property prices experienced some decline. The worst affected in this slump were Sydney and Melbourne, Australia's two biggest cities. Conversely, after 2019, Sydney and Melbourne experienced a much larger price gain than their regional areas. 

The rest of Victoria and NSW are evidently less susceptible to factors such as interest rates and global markets, as they remain comparably stable over time.

Price distribution of units, townhouses and free-standing houses

Property types under $750k sale price
Property types over $1.5m sale price

The >750k pie chart highlights that free-standing houses dominate the market in terms of number of sales. Compared to the <1.5M pie chart however, it is evident that very few townhouses and units can fetch sale prices of over $1.5M. 

This is result of townhouses and units shared living style, increased costs to the owner through bodycorporates and other fees, and smaller land sizes. Units and townhouses also are rarely demanded by investors or developers. They are usually not feasible as development properties and over time will not grow in value as a house on a considerable block of land would.
